Chittorgarh Fort

Jain Heritage Trail

A Spiritual Journey Through Time

1. Kirti Stambh (Tower of Fame)

Standing tall at 22 meters, this 12th-century monument is dedicated to Lord Adinath, the first Tirthankara. Built by a wealthy Jain merchant, Jijaji Bhagerwala, the tower is a marvel of Solanki architecture. Every inch of its surface is intricately carved with images of Tirthankaras and celestial beings.

2. Satbis Deori Temple

The largest Jain temple complex in the fort, named "Satbis" (Seven and Twenty) after the 27 auxiliary shrines that surround the main sanctum. Originally built in the 15th century, it features exquisite marble carvings and a serene atmosphere perfect for meditation and prayer.

Visitor Tip: The best time to experience the trail is during sunrise or sunset, when the golden light illuminates the ancient marble, bringing the carvings to life.
